## Overview

REI's Let's Camp campaign focused on getting campers, new and seasoned, excited about the camping season. It was a great time to pick up new gear, replace what's worn out, or upgrade depending on how you camp. But the campaign was also about something bigger: the people you bring along on your trips.

![Image caption placeholder](/images/work/rei/lets_camp_id/lets_camp_id_primary.png)
*Caption placeholder*

## The Challenge

The prior two campaign cycles didn't resonate with shoppers and lacked an identity they could recognize and get excited by. REI needed something to signal to shoppers that camping season was coming and it's time to start prepping.

## My Approach

Create an identity that integrates REI's long-standing brand and pulls from its rich history of illustration. It needed to be flexible enough to exist across several applications (print, web, and more), work in one color or with texture, and never impose on REI's current identity.

*Caption placeholder*

## Outcome

- Campaign sales were up 15% over prior cycles.
- Social media engagement increased 10% across the camping season.
- Lasting impression with shoppers frequently asking for promotional items like stickers, mugs, towels, water bottles, and key chains.
- The identity became something people wanted to take with them, not just shop through.
- REI stores saw increased foot traffic during the campaign launch windows.
- The flexible identity system scaled cleanly across in-store signage, email, web banners, and print without requiring custom adaptations.

## Reflections

Designing an identity with nationwide exposure means thinking about every context at once. It has to hold up at every scale, from a hang tag to a billboard. Someone should recognize it in passing without
  needing explanation. It needs to feel like REI, not like a separate brand competing for attention. Regional audiences across the country need to connect with it the same way, regardless of how or where they
  camp. And when production constraints limit you to one color or a small format, it still has to work. Most importantly, it should invite people in, not feel exclusive to experienced campers.
